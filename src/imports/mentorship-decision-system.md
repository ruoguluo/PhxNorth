STEP 1
Title: Identify the Core Career Direction

Description:

The system interprets the user's initial question using the existing 5D profile and guided prompts to identify the likely direction of the decision.

At this stage the goal is not solving the problem yet, but determining:

• the decision type
• the potential objective
• the user's current context

The accuracy of this identification improves continuously as the user's 5D profile becomes richer.

Dynamic example panel (left side):

User Question
"I am not sure what my next career step should be."

System Identification

Possible direction tags appear:

Goal type
• Career Promotion
• Job Change
• Career Transition

Context tags

• Team Management
• Business Growth
• Strategy / Leadership

Constraint signals

• Family responsibility
• Limited travel flexibility

Outcome of Step 1

The system identifies the **likely career objective** and confirms the primary decision direction.

---

STEP 2
Title: Decompose the Question into a Structured Pathway

Description:

Once the high-level objective is identified, the system breaks the large question into smaller, structured sub-questions.

This step gradually transforms the vague career concern into a clear decision pathway.

Dynamic example panel:

Stage 1 — Situation Snapshot

Understanding the user's current role and environment.

Examples:

• Considering promotion
• Considering job change
• Current responsibility: team management
• Current focus: business growth

Stage 2 — Identify Bottlenecks

The system asks deeper questions to reveal real obstacles.

Examples:

• Low visibility within organization
• Lack of ownership of major projects
• Limited exposure to strategic decision making

Stage 3 — Clarify the Real Challenge

The system synthesizes inputs into a problem statement.

Example system insight:

"The key challenge is not simply finding a new role, but increasing leadership visibility and ownership of high-impact projects to support promotion."

Output of Step 2

A structured mentorship blueprint:

1. Define viable career direction
2. Build strategic project ownership
3. Position for promotion or targeted opportunities

---

STEP 3
Title: Match the Right Mentor and Manage the Mentorship Process

Description:

Based on the structured problem and the user's 5D profile, the platform identifies mentors whose experience, decision style, and behavioral patterns align with the user's needs.

Before the mentorship begins, both sides already understand:

• the structured question
• the stage objective
• the expected session agenda

During the mentorship process, the system:

• tracks progress across the structured pathway
• evaluates outcome effectiveness
• identifies potential mismatches

If a mentor is not the best fit for a particular sub-problem, the system can recommend a new mentor better aligned with the user's 5D profile.

5D PROFILE LEARNING LOOP (Section below)

Title:
The 5D Identity Profile Continuously Evolves

Description:

Every interaction within the system — questions, decisions, mentorship discussions, and outcomes — enriches the user's 5D identity profile.

The platform learns:

• decision patterns
• communication preferences
• risk calibration
• behavioral tendencies in professional environments

This continuously improving identity model powers three major systems:

1. Precision mentorship matching
2. Global talent mobility opportunities
3. Enterprise advisory and business consulting
