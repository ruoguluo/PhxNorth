– Wanting better work-life balance

– Team management

The system asks the user to identify perceived career barriers.

• Low visibility within organization
• Lack of ownership of major projects
• Skill gaps for the next role
• Organizational politics or misalignment
• Family responsibilities affecting availability
• Uncertainty about direction

Step 3 — Work Preference Mapping
The system identifies what type of work the user enjoys most.

Top preferred work styles (ranked):

• Solving complex strategic problems
• Leading teams and developing people
• Driving external partnerships or negotiations
• Deep professional expertise and analysis
• Building new initiatives from zero

Red lines (things the user wants to avoid):

• Heavy travel
• Constant new market expansion
• High uncertainty performance pressure
• Frequent cross-timezone work

Step 4 — Detect Aspirations vs Constraints

The system identifies possible conflicts between career aspirations and life constraints.

Example output:

User aspirations
• Promotion to Director level
• Higher compensation

User constraints
• Newborn child and increased family responsibility
• Cannot travel frequently
• Prefer predictable schedule

System insight:

"Your desired promotion path may conflict with roles requiring heavy travel or new market expansion. We will prioritize leadership roles focused on existing business growth or internal strategic projects."

The system reframes the vague question into a clear strategic challenge:

"Your next step is not simply a job change.
The key challenge is building visibility and ownership of high-impact projects that support promotion while maintaining family constraints."

Stage 1 — Define viable promotion paths

Stage 2 — Build internal influence and project ownership

Stage 3 — Position for promotion or targeted external opportunities
Prepare promotion case, narrative, and negotiation strategy.
